G Suite Basic and Business Editions Compared

By: Michael Daniels, Magpies Consulting
As a small to medium-sized business leader, are you struggling to choose which Google G Suite subscription is best for your organization? Specifically, are you trying to determine why you should select the more feature-rich G Suite Business edition over the less costly version G Suite Basic edition? Google provides clear and transparent guidance on the capabilities provided for each subscription, which can be found at this link. The goal of this article is to summarize the difference between each and, through simplified real-world scenarios, help you determine which subscription is most appropriate for your business.
Through an in-depth review of G Suite documentation, our team at Magpies Consulting has determined that there are seven enhanced capabilities the Business option provides that are not available in the Basic option. Depending on your organization, these features may be critical to your success. We describe each below in terms of how they would be used and the impact they may have on your organization
Collaboration on documents both within and outside the organization - Does your organization share documents that teams collaborate on? Internally a department such as human resources may want a central location that stores all applicant resumes providing easy access for all department staff. Other departments may want a location that would allow team members to share documents related to their projects.
This could increase the speed and efficiency of the project through more seamless collaboration. Externally you may want to work together on documents with a customer, a partner, or a supplier. In this instance, it may be the sharing of project plans allowing each party to quickly review and update the status of activities. Capability - This feature, called Shared Drive, is part of the Google Drive product. The solution allows administrators and users, with the proper authorizations, to create document folders that can be shared with users inside and outside the organization. Targeted Benefits - Improved file sharing, especially when there are a large number of files; easier collaboration across users creating shorter times to complete tasks.
